Tide Stretch

Live radio, slowed down by the actual sea. Pick a real tide gauge — Killybegs, Dublin, Lowestoft, anywhere in Ireland or the UK — and the water level sets how much the sound is stretched: tide out, the radio smears into an ambient drone; tide in, the voices slowly return. Works with any audio file too. Start the radio (or drop a file in), press play, switch the live tide on, and leave it running.

Tide signal

Live mode reads a real tide gauge every 5 minutes — UK stations from the Environment Agency, Irish from the Marine Institute, Australian (Queensland) from the Queensland Government. The station's last 25 hours calibrate its high/low range: high water = stretch at "low", tide fully out = stretch at "high". Teal line = actual stretch over the last two minutes.

Sound engine: paulstretch by Nasca Octavian Paul, running in real time in your browser. Tide data: UK Environment Agency · Ireland Marine Institute · Queensland Queensland Government. Radio: Highland Radio, Letterkenny, Co. Donegal — streamed directly from their public stream to your browser; nothing is rebroadcast. Everything runs client-side; no server, no tracking.
