TIDAL WAVE

local radio, slowed down by the local sea

Every white dot on this map is a real tide gauge, reporting the height of its sea right now.

Tap one and its nearest local radio station starts to play — stretched in time by the water. As the tide goes out, the voices smear into a slow ambient drone, up to 30× slower. As it comes back in, they gather themselves back towards normal speed. The change happens on tidal time: leave it playing.

Stretching a live broadcast means you're always listening to the recent past — the ticker below shows how far behind the world you are, and ● REC samples up to 60 seconds of it as a WAV.

The little sea in the logo is the tide you're listening to: the water level is the real water level, and it moves at the speed of the sound.

1,305 gauges · 94 countries · every one paired with a genuinely local station. Sound: paulstretch, running in your browser. Nothing is recorded or rebroadcast — streams play straight from each station to you. Made in Donegal.
